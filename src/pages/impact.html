Impact & Success Stories

Tenda Wema Community Project has grown into a beacon of hope and transformation for vulnerable refugee and host communities in Kenya. Since its founding in 2014, the organization has made significant strides in uplifting lives through education, livelihoods, and socio-economic empowerment. Below, we highlight the impact and success stories that demonstrate the profound difference Tenda Wema has made.

1. Education: Admission of 300 Students and Counting

Tenda Wema Community School, established in Kitengela, Kajiado County, Kenya, has become a vital resource for the local community, enrolling over 300 students since January 2023. The school’s continuous growth is a testament to its high-quality, Christian-based teaching methods that not only emphasize academic excellence but also the moral and ethical development of the students. The curriculum integrates Christian values, creating an environment that fosters respect, discipline, and community spirit. The success of Tenda Wema’s educational program can be attributed to the holistic approach that combines academic rigor with a nurturing environment, making it attractive to both refugee and host community families. As the school grows, its ability to admit more students continues to expand, creating opportunities for children from underprivileged backgrounds to receive quality education. The increasing demand for enrollment reflects the school’s strong reputation within the community, with many parents seeking a stable, values-based educational experience for their children.

2. Food Donations to Students' Families in Need

Recognizing the financial struggles that many families in the community face, Tenda Wema has gone beyond education by providing food donations to the families of students in need. Since its establishment, Tenda Wema has consistently supported vulnerable households by distributing food packages that ensure children can continue attending school without the distraction of hunger. These donations are not limited to school children but also extend to their families, allowing the organization to address the broader issue of food insecurity. In 2023 alone, Tenda Wema provided food donations to over 150 families, ensuring that children and their households have access to basic nutrition. This initiative has been instrumental in keeping students in school and improving their academic performance, as hunger is one of the main barriers to learning. Many of these families rely on irregular income or are part of low-wage labor, and the food donations alleviate some of the financial pressure, allowing them to focus on other aspects of their lives, such as work and self-improvement.

3. Support for Livelihoods through VSLAs and Table Banking

Tenda Wema’s impact extends beyond education, as the organization has been actively involved in empowering community members economically. One of the key initiatives that has made a significant difference is the introduction of Village Savings and Loan Associations (VSLAs) and table banking. These programs have provided vulnerable individuals, particularly women, with the opportunity to save money, access credit, and invest in small businesses or family needs. By organizing community members into savings groups, Tenda Wema has enabled individuals to pool their resources and lend money to each other at low interest rates, a critical step toward financial independence. So far, the VSLAs have supported over 200 livelihoods, with many participants using the loans to start or expand small businesses such as grocery stalls, tailoring shops, and poultry farming. These businesses not only generate income but also contribute to the local economy by providing essential goods and services. Table banking has been another tool used by Tenda Wema to empower community members, with group members meeting regularly to save and borrow money directly from their collective pool. This system allows members to access credit without the need for formal financial institutions, which can be inaccessible to refugees and those without collateral. With the help of these programs, many families have been able to improve their economic situation, pay for their children’s education, and invest in household improvements.

4. Success through the Accelerated Education Program

Tenda Wema’s Accelerated Education Program (AEP) has been a lifeline for students who have experienced disruptions in their education due to displacement or other challenges. This program is designed to help students who have missed several years of school catch up on their education through an intensive, fast-paced curriculum. By condensing several years of learning into a shorter time frame, the program allows students to re-enter the formal education system at the appropriate level for their age. The AEP has seen remarkable success, with over 50 students having completed the program and successfully transitioning to the next level of education. These students, who would have otherwise been left behind due to their disrupted education, are now on track to complete their schooling and pursue higher education or vocational training. The success stories emerging from this program demonstrate the resilience and determination of the students, many of whom have gone on to excel in their studies despite the challenges they have faced. For example, some of the program’s graduates have been admitted to secondary schools or vocational training centers, where they continue to thrive academically and personally. The accelerated program’s flexibility and individualized approach ensure that each student’s unique needs are met, giving them the tools they need to succeed in both their education and future careers.
